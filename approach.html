Problem Based Language Learning

We use proven techniques to overcome language challenges and achieve communication goals.

We set up the problems, you solve them with language.

Our language classes focus on the essence of communication: getting your point across.
While we work on standard vocabulary and grammar, our teachers emphasize using language in context
by providing full dialogue demonstrations with relevant situations and workplace problems.

Learner Responsibilities

  • Motivation to attend class and engage online
  • Clear goals for communication in second language
  • Good understanding of workplace tasks and demands
  • Confidence in ability to grow
  • Willingness to speak and play with new language

Teacher Responsibilities

  • Develop a positive, supportive learning environment
  • Pose authentic communication problems for work and for life
  • Expose learners to native speaker cultural norms
  • Demonstate effective language solutions based on common communication problems
  • Support learning with corrective demonstration and feedback

FAQ

It depends. Language learning progress is most effective when all parties involed - client, learner, teacher - are striving towards the same ends. Within a ten week program, motivated individuals can raise their Cambridge Standard level by at least one rank (A1 to A2).

We measure success the same way as you do: with observable results. Just as we prefer authentic language tasks, we also prefer authentic language tests involving real-world challenges. Our pre and post assessments reflect this priority.

We usually use written language after a strong foundation of spoken language has been built. While written communication is indeed important, we want to be sure that everything a learner reads or writes is based on a solid phonic and cultural background.
